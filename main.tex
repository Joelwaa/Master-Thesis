\section{Measurable functions of information}
Treatment of \parencite[Prop.~5.18,5.21.]{Arandjelovic2025} leading up to  \parencite[Thm.~5.23.]{Arandjelovic2025}.
Conceptually: Reduction to countably generated sigma-fields and a link to \parencite{Buehler2018DeepHedging}. More precisely: Understanding \parencite[Prop.~5.17]{Arandjelovic2025} as the measure theoretic statement that the uncountably generated market information \(\sigma-\)algebra can, under certain assumptions, be reduced to a countably generated one. \parencite[Prop.~5.21]{Arandjelovic2025} then shows that the latter implies that for real-valued maps, we can find a countable sub-sigma algebra for which the map is also measurable. 
\parencite[Prop.~5.23]{Arandjelovic2025} then combines the insights about measurability and Orlitz density of neural networks to approximate maps \(g:(S,\mathcal{S})\rightarrow(\mathbb
R\,\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})\) that are measurable with respect to market generated filtration \(\mathcal{S}\) (which is uncountably generated in general). This generalizes results from \parencite{Buehler2018DeepHedging}, in particular the idea to approximate trading strategies measurable with respect to finitely generated market-sigma-algebra to the uncountably generated case.
